# Off Grid AI - Mission & Vision

## Positioning (the one-liner)

**AWS for AI. Open source, and built on open source.** So your AI usage is reliable, secure, governed, and compliant by default.

It is the bridge between what is already inside the enterprise (its data, its context, its processes, its people) and the fast-rising intelligence of frontier models.

Designed so non-technical people can codify what they do and multiply their output. The one-stop platform: set the organization's rules, policies, guardrails, and knowledge once, and everyone builds governed AI on top.

---

## Mission

Turn every organization into an **intelligent enterprise**, running on AI that is reliable, secure, and compliant by default.

Make it usable by **every employee, not just engineers**. Anyone describes what they need in plain language and gets back a working, governed workflow, tested safely in a sandbox before it touches anything real.

Keep it **open and transparent**. Every AI action is observable, attributable, and reversible, and the platform is open source, so anyone can inspect it and trust it.

## Vision

Real change reaches the **population** through the enterprises that serve it. So the way to have impact at that scale is to make those enterprises intelligent.

Intelligence woven into how they already operate, working with the systems they already run, as-is, without asking them to change a thing. Built on open foundations any enterprise can inspect, trust, and run for itself.

## The gap we close

Individual productivity with AI is already solved. Any one person gets more done with a chatbot. Harnessing that at the enterprise level is not solved. That is the gap.

An enterprise's value is its moat: the context, the data, and the processes inside it. The outside world is moving fast, with frontier models getting better every month. The enterprise needs a way to put that outside intelligence to work on its own moat, without handing the moat to someone else and without waiting on a platform team.

Off Grid AI closes that gap three ways, and the three compound:

1. **Let non-technical people build.** A person describes a workflow in plain English. The system inherits the org's rules, data, connectors, policies, and guardrails automatically, and hands back their own ecosystem, with human-in-the-loop, review, and reports. This is what the Console does today.
2. **Run intelligence at the edge.** An MDM only governs a device. Run AI on-device, privately, so personal information never leaves it, and you can use the context that never reaches a server: nudges in the moment, and knowledge routed to the right person by RBAC.
3. **Consolidate knowledge into a brain.** Watch the screen, understand the standard operating procedure, and codify it. The knowledge in people's heads becomes something the org owns and reuses.

The edge captures the context and the SOPs. The brain consolidates them. The builder turns them into governed work. The moat gets amplified, governed by default, on infrastructure the enterprise controls.

## How it works

    data  ->  gateway  ->  pipelines  ->  agents / apps  ->  compliance & regulations

Every piece you would otherwise wire together yourself already exists here and is connected: a gateway to the models, evals, guardrails, PII masking, data pipelines, audit, lineage, knowledge bases. AWS meant you stopped assembling servers. Off Grid AI means you stop assembling AI infrastructure. It works out of the box, on your own servers or in your cloud.

It is set-once, use-everywhere. An administrator defines the organization's rules, workflows, policies, guardrails, observability, data lineage, and knowledge bases one time. From then on, every employee and every agent inherits them. Nobody re-implements governance. Nobody works around it.

## What we enable

Non-technical people build workflows, agents, apps, loops, and harnesses in a secure, compliant, governed way, **without the enterprise changing anything.** Off Grid AI is a drop-in system that connects to whatever they already run and makes it intelligent. No rip-and-replace. No lock-in. No giving up control.

## The thesis, plainly

Governance, compliance, and regulatory controls are table stakes. For any enterprise, they just have to be there. They are not the pitch.

The pitch is the moat and the speed of the outside world. Every enterprise sits on a moat: its data, its people, its processes, its reach. The world is innovating fast. The enterprise needs a way to put that innovation to work on its own moat, in a controlled and contained environment, without giving anything up.

That is how an enterprise reaches its full potential: it amplifies its own moat, governed by default, on infrastructure it owns.
